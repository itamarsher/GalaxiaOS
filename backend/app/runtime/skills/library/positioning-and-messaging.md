# Positioning & Messaging

Positioning is the upstream decision every piece of marketing inherits. Get it sharp and the
rest of GTM gets easier; get it fuzzy and no amount of content saves it.

## Workflow
1. **Ground it in evidence.** Pull real customer language from calls (`crm_contact_timeline`),
   win/loss (`win-loss-analysis`), and competitor claims (`web_search`). Positioning is discovered
   from the market, not invented in a room.
2. **Fill the positioning canvas:**
   - *Target* — the specific segment that feels the pain most.
   - *Alternative* — what they use today (often a spreadsheet or nothing, not a competitor).
   - *Unique value* — the one thing we do that the alternative can't.
   - *Proof* — why that claim is believable.
3. **Write the one-liner:** "For [target] who [need], [product] is the [category] that [unique
   value], unlike [alternative]." Sharpen until it's specific enough to exclude someone.
4. **Derive the message hierarchy:** the headline promise, 3 supporting pillars, and the proof
   under each. This is what content, sales, and the site all draw from.
5. **Pressure-test.** Would a target customer nod? Would a non-target self-select out? If it
   appeals to everyone, it persuades no one — narrow it.
6. **Publish internally.** `update_company_playbook` with the positioning + message hierarchy so
   every role uses the same words. `write_memory` (type `learning`) the core insight.

## Decision framework — narrow vs. broad
When in doubt, narrow the target. Sharp positioning for a specific buyer beats vague appeal to
all buyers; you can widen later from a position of strength.

## Definition of done
- Canvas completed from real customer language; one-liner specific enough to exclude.
- Message hierarchy published to the playbook for the whole fleet to reuse.

## Common failure modes
- **Positioning by wishful thinking.** If it's not grounded in customer words, it won't resonate.
- **"For everyone."** The fastest way to persuade no one.
- **Living only in one agent's head.** If it's not in the playbook, GTM fragments.
