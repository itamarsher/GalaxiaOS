# Influencer & Creator Partnership

A creator partnership works when their audience is genuinely your ICP and the collaboration
is authentic. This playbook vets, briefs, and measures it so budget maps to outcomes.

## Workflow
1. **Vet audience fit, not follower count.** `web_search` the creator's audience, engagement
   quality, and past brand work. A 5k-follower niche fit beats a 500k generic reach.
2. **Check reputation and brand safety.** Look for controversy or misaligned values —
   `flag_legal_risk` if there's anything that could expose the company. This is an external
   association; treat it like one.
3. **Budget and terms.** Any payment/gifting is real spend — `request_budget` and define
   deliverables, usage rights, and disclosure (FTC-style "#ad") in writing. `check_compliance`
   on disclosure requirements.
4. **Brief for authenticity.** Give the creator the pain and proof, not a word-for-word script —
   audiences punish inauthentic reads. Provide assets via `generate_image`/`draft_document`.
5. **Track a unique attribution path** (code/link) so results are measurable. `log_lead` /
   `crm_save_deal` on inbound.
6. **Measure and decide.** `record_metric` for reach, referred leads, and CAC vs. other channels.
   `write_memory` (type `learning`) whether this creator type is worth repeating.

## Decision framework — fit over reach
Choose the creator whose audience overlaps your ICP most, even if smaller. Reach without fit
is spend without return.

## Definition of done
- Audience fit and brand safety vetted; disclosure compliant; attribution in place.
- CAC vs. channels recorded; repeat decision grounded in outcomes.

## Common failure modes
- **Buying reach, not fit.** Big numbers, wrong audience, no conversion.
- **Skipping disclosure.** "#ad" isn't optional; non-disclosure is a legal/brand risk.
- **Over-scripting.** Kills the authenticity that makes creators work.
