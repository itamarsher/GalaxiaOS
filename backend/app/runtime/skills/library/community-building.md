# Community Building

A real community compounds: members support each other, advocate, and surface insight. This
playbook grows one with intent, not by opening an empty channel and hoping.

## Workflow
1. **Define the community's job.** Support, advocacy, feedback, or belonging — pick the primary
   one. A community with no clear purpose stays empty. `write_memory` (type `experiment`) the goal.
2. **Seed before scaling.** Recruit a small core of genuinely engaged users (promoters from
   `nps-and-testimonial-collection`). Ten active members beat a thousand silent ones.
3. **Give members a reason to return** — exclusive insight, early access, direct line to the
   team, peer help. Set a content/ritual cadence (`schedule_social_post`, `send_chat_message`
   in a `start_chat_channel`).
4. **Show up authentically.** The team participates as humans, answers questions, and elevates
   member voices. Extractive, broadcast-only "communities" die.
5. **Turn community into insight and advocacy.** Feed recurring questions to `list_feature_requests`
   and content ideas to `content-marketing-calendar`; invite advocates into referrals.
6. **Measure engagement, not size.** `record_metric` for active members, contributions, and
   retention lift among members vs. non-members. `write_memory` (type `learning`) what drives activity.

## Decision framework — depth over headcount
Optimize for active participation, not member count. A dead 5,000-person channel signals a
dead product; a lively 50-person one is an asset.

## Definition of done
- Clear purpose, seeded core, a reason to return, authentic team presence.
- Engagement (not size) measured; community insight fed back to product/marketing.

## Common failure modes
- **Empty-room launch.** Opening a channel with no seeded core and no purpose.
- **Broadcast, not conversation.** Extractive communities wither.
- **Vanity headcount.** Size without engagement is worse than no community.
