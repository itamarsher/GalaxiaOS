# Board Meeting Prep

A board meeting is for decisions and alignment, not status theater. This playbook prepares
materials that inform the board honestly and use their time on what matters.

## Workflow
1. **Set the purpose.** What decisions or input do you need from the board this meeting? Design the
   agenda around those, not around reciting activity. `write_memory` (type `experiment`) the goals.
2. **Send materials in advance.** Prepare a concise deck/memo (`draft_document`) with the real numbers
   — metrics, financials (`monthly-financial-close`, `runway-and-burn-analysis`), progress vs. OKRs.
   Send ahead so the meeting is discussion, not reading.
3. **Lead with the truth, including bad news.** Surface risks and misses early and plainly. Boards
   forgive problems disclosed honestly; they lose trust over problems hidden until they explode.
4. **Frame decisions with options.** For each decision needed, present the situation, the options with
   trade-offs, and your recommendation. Boards decide better with a clear recommendation to react to.
5. **Prepare for hard questions.** List the weak points and have honest answers and asks ready.
   Bring specific asks (intros, expertise, approvals) — boards want to help.
6. **Capture and follow through.** Record decisions and actions; `write_memory` (type `result`);
   `dispatch_tasks` on the follow-ups. A decision with no follow-through erodes credibility.

## Decision framework — decisions over updates
Spend board time on the few decisions only they can help with; relegate status to the pre-read.
Trust is built by candor about problems, not by polished good news.

## Definition of done
- Agenda built around decisions/input needed; honest pre-read sent in advance.
- Bad news surfaced plainly; decisions framed with options and a recommendation; follow-ups tracked.

## Common failure modes
- **Status theater.** Burning the meeting reciting activity instead of deciding.
- **Hiding bad news.** The fastest way to lose a board's trust.
- **No recommendation.** Options without a point of view make boards do your thinking.
