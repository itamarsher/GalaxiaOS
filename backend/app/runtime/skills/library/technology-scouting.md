# Technology Scouting

New technology is a stream of hype and genuine opportunity. This playbook evaluates emerging tech
for real fit — so the company adopts what creates advantage and ignores what merely trends.

## Workflow
1. **Start from a need or bet.** Scout against a real problem ("how do we cut inference cost?") or a
   strategic bet — not "what's new." Aimless scouting collects demos, not decisions.
2. **Survey the options.** `web_search` for approaches, tools, and their maturity. Capture what each
   actually does, its maturity/adoption, and its cost — from evidence, not vendor claims.
3. **Assess fit on real criteria:** does it solve our problem, is it mature enough to depend on, what's
   the switching/lock-in cost, and does it fit our stack and skills? A powerful tool that doesn't fit
   is a liability.
4. **Distinguish hype from substance.** Ask what it concretely does better and who's successfully in
   production with it. Early tech carries risk; weigh advantage against immaturity honestly.
5. **Prototype before committing.** For promising candidates, `dispatch_task` a small time-boxed spike
   rather than a big bet. Evidence from a prototype beats a spec sheet.
6. **Recommend.** `write_memory` (type `learning`) the evaluation; `create_report` /
   `request_decision` for adoption calls with material cost or lock-in.

## Decision framework — advantage vs. risk
Adopt when the concrete advantage clearly outweighs immaturity and switching cost. Bleeding-edge
tech can be a moat or a trap; a time-boxed prototype is the cheapest way to tell which.

## Definition of done
- Scouting tied to a real need/bet; options surveyed from evidence; fit assessed on concrete criteria.
- Hype separated from substance; promising options prototyped; adoption calls routed for decision.

## Common failure modes
- **Shiny-object scouting.** Evaluating tech with no problem to solve.
- **Vendor-claim trust.** Assess real maturity and production use, not marketing.
- **Big bet, no prototype.** Committing before cheap evidence exists.
